The Sicilian-American Flag

Design and Meaning

This version of the Sicilian-American flag draws on centuries of Sicilian history, Christian faith, and transatlantic migration. The three legs represent the three corners of Sicily, symbolizing unity across distance and diaspora. The Baroque-style sunburst radiates from a central cross—reflecting Sicily’s deep Catholic heritage and the spiritual light that has guided generations of Sicilians across oceans.

The deep blue field represents maritime traditions, freedom, and the transatlantic journey. The alternating blue and white stripes signify Sicilian integration into American life while honoring both nations’ legacies. The bold red stripe in the center echoes Palermo and symbolizes strength and sacrifice.
