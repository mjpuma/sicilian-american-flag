Introduction

This Sicilian-American flag brings together three anchors of identity: place, faith, and journey. A USA-blue canton carries a gold cross‑Trinacria, while the field is composed of Palermo‑red and white stripes—nine in total, honoring Sicily’s nine historic provinces. The design reads instantly American in layout, unmistakably Sicilian in meaning.

The Cross‑Trinacria (Gold)

The Trinacria—three running legs—comes from the island’s ancient Greek name Trinakría (“three capes”: Peloro, Boeo, Passero). Rendered here in a restrained, heraldic form, it expresses motion, resilience, and the triangular geography of Sicily. The cross at its heart affirms the island’s deep Christian heritage from late antiquity through the Norman and Baroque eras. Colored gold/yellow, the emblem evokes Sicilian sunlight, wheat and abundance, and the gold grounds of the island’s celebrated mosaics.

USA‑Blue Canton

Placed in the American position (upper hoist), the canton declares the promise that drew generations across the Atlantic: liberty, law, and opportunity. Its blue converses with the U.S. flag while providing clear contrast for the gold device.

Palermo‑Red & White Stripes (Nine)

The field carries nine horizontal stripes—for Palermo, Messina, Catania, Agrigento, Trapani, Siracusa, Enna, Caltanissetta, and Ragusa. Palermo red draws on the capital’s civic tradition and memory of revolution; white introduces clarity and hope. Together they echo the cadence of the American flag while remaining rooted in Sicilian color symbolism and maritime history.

How to Read the Flag

From hoist to fly, the eye moves from the canton (origin and faith carried forward), across the stripes (the people and provinces), to the open field (the ongoing journey). The balance keeps the dialogue between Sicily and America alive in a single, cohesive banner.

Symbolism at a Glance

ElementMeaning
USA‑blue canton Liberty, law, and the American journey that welcomed Sicilians across the Atlantic.
Gold cross‑Trinacria Three capes of Sicily (motion and resilience) united by the Cross (faith and renewal); gold for sun, wheat, and mosaic light.
Nine stripes (Palermo red & white) Nine historic provinces; a dialogue with the American flag’s rhythm; clarity, courage, and community.
