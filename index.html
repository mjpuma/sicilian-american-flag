Introduction

The Sicilian-American flag blends the island’s enduring Catholic and cultural heritage with the freedoms secured across the Atlantic. The deep blue canton represents liberty and the American journey—across both space and time. A white Baroque-style sunburst radiates with divine light, recalling altarpieces in Palermo, Ragusa, and Noto. A small cross at its center symbolizes Sicily’s deep Christian roots dating to the earliest days of the faith.

Below the canton, nine stripes represent the nine historic provinces of Sicily—Palermo, Messina, Catania, Agrigento, Trapani, Siracusa, Enna, Caltanissetta, and Ragusa. The central red stripe pays tribute to Palermo, the heart of the island and of past revolutions. These stripes also evoke Sicily’s maritime traditions—fishing, trade, and transatlantic migration that brought so many to the American shores in search of freedom and opportunity.
