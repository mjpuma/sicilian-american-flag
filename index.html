Sicilian‑American Flag

“Uniting Sicilian faith and ingenuity with American opportunity, this flag celebrates our journey across seas, across centuries, and toward freedom.”

This modern flag draws on rich layers of Sicilian history and Christian symbolism. A deep navy canton reflects the American ideal of liberty, while also invoking Sicily’s ancient maritime culture — from Greek seafarers and Phoenician traders to generations of fishermen and immigrants who crossed the Atlantic. In the canton’s center, a Baroque-style white sunburst radiates with divine light, drawing inspiration from church altarpieces in Palermo, Ragusa, and Noto. A white cross at the center signals Christian heritage, unity, and hope.

The body of the flag features nine horizontal stripes — a clear tribute to Sicily’s nine historic provinces: Palermo, Catania, Messina, Agrigento, Trapani, Siracusa, Enna, Ragusa, and Caltanissetta. The red central stripe honors Palermo, long the cultural and political heart of the island. The blue and white stripes reflect both Sicilian and American identities — sea, sky, faith, and freedom — and the journey between two homelands.
