The Sicilian-American Flag celebrates the vibrant cultural identity and values of Sicilian-Americans, blending the legacy of Sicily with the ideals of the United States.

Symbol Meaning
9 Stripes (5 blue, 4 white) Represents the 9 provinces of Sicily: Palermo, Catania, Messina, Trapani, Siracusa, Agrigento, Enna, Caltanissetta, and Ragusa
Red Central Stripe Represents Palermo, the historical and political heart of Sicily
Blue Field (Canton) Symbolizes the maritime journey of Sicilians across time and space, evoking freedom and connection to the sea
Three Legs with Radiating Sunburst and Cross A reinterpretation of the Trinacria, honoring the Holy Trinity and Sicily’s long Christian heritage; the white sunburst with 9 major rays evokes Baroque altarpieces from churches in Palermo, Ragusa, and Noto. The Cross symbolizes light, sacrifice, and spiritual heritage

Historical Baroque influences in this flag’s center come from sunburst motifs seen in church art such as:

  • Palermo Cathedral
  • Ragusa Cathedral
  • Noto Cathedral
